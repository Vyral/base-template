Two new businesses—a bar and a barbershop—have opened up recently in Manlius that I want to explore and share with you today.

So many cool things are happening on the development side of Manlius that I wanted to take a moment today to spotlight a couple new businesses.
The first is Saving Face Barbershop, where I spoke with barber and co-owner Taylor Horseman. Located at 127 East Seneca St., this barbershop has been in business at their new location for about four months.
According to Taylor, what makes their establishment different is that their employees have been at their jobs for a long time and have passion in what they do. It’s more of an art to them. With the community-type atmosphere they present, they intend for it to be a place where men can hang out and build friendships in addition to getting a haircut.
While you wait for a haircut, you can watch TV, play shuffleboard, or pour yourself a cup of coffee. They like to be versatile and able to accommodate anyone, so they perform many different types of cuts, include straight-razor shaves and full facial treatments.
Next, I stopped in at All Who Wander, a craft beer bar and grill at 315 Fayette St. There I spoke with co-owners Dan Chapman and Joe Ori. In their words, the name “All Who Wander” speaks to the experience of finding your place to enjoy a nice craft beverage, a grilled cheese sandwich, and a nice social experience. For them, it’s as simple as taking the products that they love and sharing them with their customers.
I highly recommend that you visit either of these locations.
Speaking of products, they not only have wine and many regional beers on tap, but also on-tap nitro coffee. If you haven’t heard of this new trend, nitro coffee is cold-brew coffee infused with nitrogen so it pours out of a tap just like a Guinness would. It adds a silky, smooth flavor that’s nothing short of delicious on a warm day.
All of the craft beers they serve are hand selected by Dan and Joe. They consider it a very important part of their job to represent the beer itself and share with people the way certain beers are supposed to taste. Furthermore, the history and story of the beer brings more to the drinking experience (I call this “knowledge-based drinking”). One of the fulfilling things about their work is sharing stories back and forth with their customers.
Whether it’s for a haircut or a pint of ale, I highly recommend that you visit either of these locations in the near future. It’s great for Manlius, and I look forward to featuring more local businesses in the near future.
