Is there a difference between real estate agents and real estate companies?

It’s a question that, unfortunately, most people never ask. How do I know that? Look at all the unsold homes that are on the market! If all agents were alike and trained the same way, we would never have any unsold homes sitting on the market for more than 30 days.

In a recent survey, I asked some followers of my Facebook page what they thought were some of things agents learned in licensing class. Here were their answers (all false):
  • Marketing and advertising
  • How to price a home correctly
  • Construction
  • How to stage a home to get top dollar
  • How to write a contract
  • How to sell houses
“
It’s wrong to assume anybody with 
a real estate license knows 
how to sell a house.
”


Surprised none of these apply? The truth is, we only learn federal and state laws that pertain to ownership and the transferring of real property, and ethics classes. I think the general public perceives that anybody with a real estate license knows exactly what they’re doing to sell a house. For the last 29 years, however, I’ve been - at my own expense - attending numerous conventions, seminars, and conferences in order to network and undergo training by the best and brightest minds in the industry.

I’m protecting your largest asset, so I need to know more than what you can find on the Internet. In addition, RE/MAX offers extensive training through RE/MAX University and each of our offices. RE/MAX has the most highly-trained agents in all of the real estate industry.
