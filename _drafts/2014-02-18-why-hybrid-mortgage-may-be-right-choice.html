Hybrid Mortgages: Low Interest Rates, But What Else?

Today I wanted to talk about a term that is rather new in the marketplace, “hybrid mortgages”. To help explain them, I have invited Al Ripple from Johnson bank to join us. Al explains that a hybrid mortgage is simply a new term for an adjustable rate loan with the most common ARM’s being 1, 5 and 10 years. The current rate on a one year ARM, or a one year hybrid loan, is 1.875%; as explained, that is dramatically different than 4.3% for a 30 year fixed loan! After Al told me this, I understood why these hybrid mortgages were so attractive to people.

Although the term’s rates are very low, I thought it would be important to understand how much the rate could fluctuate. Al clarified that the adjustment maximums on a one year ARM are what’s called “2 and 6.” This basically means that after the first year, the interest rate could adjust a full 2% which means you could be moving from 1.875% to 3.875% in just one year.

After that, Al states that the loan will adjust once per year thereafter for the remaining life of the loan and not to exceed 6% over the initial rate. Theoretically, in three years after the initial rate expires, Al says your rate could be as high as 7.875%. Essentially, these mortgages would be ideal for someone who was planning on moving within the next 3-5 years. As Al stated, these are perfect for corporate transferees who, as I said previously, would be only be staying for 3-5 years.
