Why Pre-Approval is Necessary When Buying a Home

We are back again, with our friend Suzi Copper, to talk a little more about how to have a smooth and successful loan process. A lot of lenders out there won't look at a buyer's financial history first, and instead just write a prequalification letter. There really is no advantage to this, however. A pre-qualification letter is basically just a letter that says "this person is qualified".

A pre-approval is different. A pre-approval involves a lender collecting information, submitting it to a bank, getting an underwriter's blessing, and getting approved. All you have to do from here is find a property and your offer is much stronger with a listing agent and a seller. Especially if you are looking for something quick, if you are pre-approved, the process is much easier. 

By cooperating up front, and providing your lender with all the info you need, you will have a great customer experience. Suzi says that her clients that have the most luck are the ones that get here everything up front. 

If you are thinking of buying anytime in the next 3 months, pre-approval is very important. A pre-approval lasts for 3 months and getting it taken care of now will allow you to be ready to write an offer right away!
