Today, Al Ripple of Johnson Bank joins us to discuss investment property, a hot topic in our market right now.

If someone is a first or second-time buyer who plans to live in half of a new investment property, will they still be required to put 20% down? The answer, of course, is it depends on the loan program they're utilizing. If they're using a conventional loan, the down payment will be 20% of the purchase price. On the other hand, if they're looking at the first-time home buyer program, you can probably get in with as low as 10% down! 


There are even better deals out there for potential investors who qualify. FHA loans require as low as 3.5% down, while VA loans require no financing if the investor chooses to live in the property.

As you can see, there are a lot of options out there if you want a tenant to pay half of your mortgage. It's a great way to get into the investment game without taking a huge risk, and we would love to chat with you about your options.
