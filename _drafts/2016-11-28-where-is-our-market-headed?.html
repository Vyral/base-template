What does the state of the market look like? Today, I’d like to talk a little bit about our current market conditions.
We’ve been experiencing one the of the best real estate markets we’ve seen in the past 10 years. We’ve seen home appreciation levels rise due to low interest rates and pent-up demand. Because of these conditions, our market is leaning toward a seller’s market.
This is also apparent when we look at the days on market, which is hovering around 60 days right now. A strong buyer’s market would be something closer to 90 days on market, while anything below 60 days would be considered a strong seller’s market. A number like 75 days on market would indicate more of a balanced market. This number can change, however, depending on the price range of properties.
This past spring and into the summer, inventory levels were very low. These low levels have pushed prices even higher. By the end of the summer, this turned around and the housing inventory is now sitting at about the same level that it was a year ago—around 6.3 months of inventory.
The greatest demand we’re seeing right now is for homes priced between $300,000 and $400,000. This demand significantly drops off for any properties over $550,000.
As we head into the fall market, we’re already seeing activity pick up. What this means for buyers is that if they see a house they’re interested in, they’ll want to get out and see it right away. Buyers should also be sure to put in a strong offer in order to stay ahead of the competition. Right now, we are seeing multiple offers in certain price points.
What this means for sellers is that now is a great time to list your home on the market. Selling your home before the holiday season will help ensure that your home doesn’t sit on the market all winter long. The Federal Reserve is also expected to raise rates by the end of the year, which makes it even more important for sellers to list their homes as soon as possible.
