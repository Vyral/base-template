Today we’re talking about the second rule of home staging: How to keep it fresh. We’ve found that the rooms that buyers are most interested in before they look at the rest of the house are the living room, master bedroom, and the kitchen.

So what can you do in those rooms to make sure that they stand out? You don’t have to spend a lot of money. A fresh coat of paint on the walls or the baseboards is an inexpensive way to freshen up those rooms.

In the kitchen, paint the cabinets instead of replacing them. Another thing you can do is update the fixtures in the kitchen to give it a new look and feel.

    Paint the cabinets instead of replacing them.



    In the master bedroom, you want to give potential buyers the space to be able to imagine themselves living in that room. Remove any personal items and repaint the room to a neutral color. To give it a little bit of dimension, put throw pillows on the bed.

    These are just a few of the tips and tricks that we can give you to freshen up your home while it’s on the market. We can talk more about how to properly stage your home in detail when you sit down with us to prepare your home for listing.
