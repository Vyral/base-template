By now, you've heard that Pennsylvania will soon require written buyer agreements before agents can show buyers a home. So, how do you have a successful buyer consultation and get those written agreements signed? Let me walk you through the three essential components.

**Step 1: Building Trust**

The first step is to hold a consultation to guide the buyer through the home-buying process. You're going to find out from them what it is that they're looking for in a home and what they're looking for in a real estate agent. During this section of your buyer consultation, you're going to work to earn the buyer's trust through asking great questions, active listening, and truly showing empathy for the situation that they're in.

It's about making a connection and understanding their needs, desires, and concerns. By doing this, you show that you genuinely care about their journey, which sets a solid foundation for a successful partnership.

**Step 2: Educating the Buyer**

The next part of your buyer consultation is going to be education. It's important that you first learn about the buyer before you move into the education section. People don't care how much you know until they know how much you care. Making sure in this part that you guide the buyer through the process using what you learned from the needs analysis will be crucial in helping you to get your buyer agreement signed.

This is where you demystify the home-buying process for them. Explain the steps, set expectations, and provide valuable insights based on your expertise. This not only empowers the buyer but also reinforces your role as a knowledgeable and reliable advisor.<br>

{% include pullquote.html %}

**Step 3: Utilizing the Written Buyer Agency Contract**

The third step is to use the written buyer agency contract provided by the State of Pennsylvania. This contract gives you all of the knowledge and language that you need to describe your fee, and all of your corresponding duties to the buyer.

This contract formalizes your relationship with the buyer, outlining your responsibilities and fees. It's an essential tool that ensures clarity and professionalism, helping to secure a mutual commitment.
