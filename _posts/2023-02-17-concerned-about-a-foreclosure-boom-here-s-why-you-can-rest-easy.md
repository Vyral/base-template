According to media reports, foreclosure filings doubled in the United States in 2022. This alarming statistic may have some people wondering if we're in for another wave of foreclosures. However, it's important to take a closer look at the data and put things into perspective.

First, while a doubling of foreclosure filings may sound like a big increase, it's important to remember that it's nowhere near the levels we saw during the housing market crash of 2008. In fact, in some areas, such as Washington County, the number of foreclosures in 2022 was incredibly low, with only eight total foreclosures for the entire year.

One reason is that **homeowners today have more equity in their homes than ever before.** Many people currently in the market have been able to take advantage of historically low interest rates to refinance their mortgages and build up their equity. This means that even if they were to face financial difficulties, they would still have a significant cushion to protect them from foreclosure.

{% include pullquote.html %}

Another factor is that **lending standards have become stricter in recent years.** Before the housing market crash, it was easy for people to obtain mortgages that they couldn't really afford. This led to a flood of foreclosures when the market turned. Today, lenders are much more careful about who they lend to and require stricter documentation and financial qualifications. This has helped to ensure that people are getting mortgages that they can truly afford and reduces the likelihood of foreclosures down the line.

All of this is good news for anyone worried about another wave of foreclosures. While it's true that there has been an increase in foreclosure filings, it's important to keep things in perspective. Homeowners today are in a much stronger financial position than they were in 2008. Even if they were to face financial difficulties, they are much less likely to lose their homes to foreclosure.

If you're an investor looking for a screaming foreclosure deal, you may be disappointed by the current market conditions. With homeowners holding more equity than ever before, it's tough to find a bargain in the foreclosure market. However, if you're a consumer worried about a foreclosure boom, you can rest easy knowing that the data suggests otherwise.
