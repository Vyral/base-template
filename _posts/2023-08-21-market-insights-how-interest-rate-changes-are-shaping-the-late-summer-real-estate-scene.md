As we find ourselves winding down in the late summer of 2023, it's time to take a closer look at the current state of the real estate market. One key factor that's been making waves in recent weeks is the fluctuating interest rates. This past week, interest rates have settled around seven and a half percent, and their influence on the market is undeniable.

The shifting interest rates have not gone unnoticed in the world of real estate transactions. A notable observation is the change in the volume and nature of transactions. The market has long been grappling with a familiar refrain â€“ low inventory. However, this scarcity of available properties has paradoxically lent a degree of stability to the market. Unlike in previous years, where prices exhibited sharp rises or falls, **the current stable conditions have created a more balanced environment for both buyers and sellers.**

With the interest rates no longer at their rock-bottom levels, the behavior of buyers has undergone a transformation. The once-flooded gates of eager buyers have slowed to a more measured pace. Affordability has become a key consideration for prospective buyers, prompting them to reevaluate their purchasing strategies. One notable strategy gaining traction is the use of contingent offers.

Despite the sometimes negative reputation contingent offers have acquired, they're not inherently detrimental. These offers hinge on the sale of the buyer's current property. **Smart sellers, in such scenarios, consider the nature of the property that needs to be sold.** Opting for the property that's easier to sell can make contingent offers a viable option worth considering.

{% include pullquote.html %}

Intriguingly, the changing interest rates have led to shifts in financing choices even after the transaction process has begun. This year, a growing number of buyers have switched from conventional loans to FHA loans mid-transaction. This switch isn't indicative of the buyer's strength or commitment but rather reflects their responsiveness to evolving financial conditions. This trend is a deviation from the past, where such changes were rare.

An interesting shift in behavior is the timing of locking in interest rates. In the recent past, buyers hurried to lock in their rates immediately upon securing a purchase agreement. Now, with interest rates displaying more variability, buyers are displaying more patience. They are closely monitoring market movements and pouncing on favorable rates when they become available. This approach demonstrates a savvy awareness of the dynamics of the real estate market.

As interest rates continue to hover around 7%, buyers are looking for ways to minimize their financial burden. One strategy that's emerging is the negotiation of seller-paid closing costs. By exploring different products and rate guidance, buyers are attempting to make their investment as financially efficient as possible. This tactic aligns with their goal of securing the best possible rates in the current interest rate climate.

As we navigate the late summer real estate landscape, the influence of interest rates on the market is undeniable. Buyers are becoming more strategic in their approach, employing contingent offers, switching financing mid-transaction, and timing their rate locks more judiciously. Meanwhile, the ever-present low inventory is contributing to a market that is more stable and less prone to the dramatic price fluctuations of previous years.
